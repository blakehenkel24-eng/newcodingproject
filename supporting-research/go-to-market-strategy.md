# SlideTheory Go-to-Market Strategy

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Product:** SlideTheory - AI-Powered Slide Generation for Strategy Consultants

---

## Executive Summary

SlideTheory is a web application that generates McKinsey/Bain-quality presentation slides in under 30 seconds. This go-to-market strategy outlines the comprehensive plan to acquire, activate, and retain strategy consultants as our primary users, while expanding to adjacent markets over time.

**Target Launch:** Q1 2026  
**Primary Goal:** 1,000 MAU within 90 days of launch  
**Revenue Goal:** $5,000 MRR by end of Q2 2026

---

## 1. Target Audience Deep Dive

### 1.1 Primary Audience: Strategy Consultants at MBB and Tier-2 Firms

**Market Size:**
- McKinsey: ~30,000 consultants globally
- BCG: ~25,000 consultants globally
- Bain: ~15,000 consultants globally
- Tier-2 (Deloitte S&O, Strategy&, Kearney, Roland Berger, etc.): ~50,000+ consultants
- **Total Addressable Market:** ~120,000+ strategy consultants

#### Persona 1: The Time-Starved Associate (Analyst/Consultant Level)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 24-28
- Tenure: 0-3 years at firm
- Workload: 60-80 hours/week
- Slide output: 10-30 slides/week

**Pain Points:**
- Blank-screen paralysis when starting new slides
- Spending 2-4 hours on slides that partners later rewrite
- Learning curve for firm-specific formatting standards
- Late nights redoing slides after feedback
- Inconsistent quality when working under pressure

**Use Cases:**
- Turning rough notes from client interviews into polished slides
- Creating first drafts for workstream updates
- Building data visualization slides from Excel outputs
- Preparing executive summaries for steering committees

**Motivations:**
- Get slides "partner-ready" faster
- Reduce rework and late nights
- Learn "what good looks like" for slide structure
- Impress managers with turnaround speed

**Where to Reach Them:**
- LinkedIn (professional networking)
- Consulting subreddits (r/consulting, r/FinancialCareers)
- Consulting podcasts (Consulting Success, The Consulting Offer)
- Business school career services

#### Persona 2: The Efficiency-Seeking Manager (Engagement Manager/Project Leader)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-34
- Tenure: 4-8 years at firm
- Workload: 50-70 hours/week
- Team size: 3-6 consultants

**Pain Points:**
- Reviewing and rewriting slides created by junior team members
- Inconsistent quality across workstreams
- Pressure to deliver high-quality output with tight deadlines
- Managing client expectations while maintaining quality

**Use Cases:**
- Rapid prototyping of storylines with the team
- Creating executive summary slides for C-suite presentations
- Standardizing output across multiple workstreams
- Building "straw man" decks for client workshops

**Motivations:**
- Improve team productivity
- Reduce time spent on slide reviews
- Maintain quality standards under time pressure
- Deliver more value to clients faster

**Where to Reach Them:**
- LinkedIn (thought leadership content)
- Consulting firm alumni networks
- Industry conferences
- Executive education programs

#### Persona 3: The Partner/Principal (Decision Maker)

**Demographics:**
- Age: 35+
- Tenure: 10+ years at firm
- Focus: Business development and client relationships
- Limited hands-on slide work but high quality expectations

**Pain Points:**
- Seeing inconsistent quality in junior consultant output
- Limited time to coach on slide craft
- Need for rapid turnaround on business development materials

**Use Cases:**
- Reviewing and approving AI-generated slides
- Using as a teaching tool for junior staff
- Creating proposals and pitch decks

**Motivations:**
- Improve firm-wide output quality
- Reduce cost per slide/client deliverable
- Free up time for higher-value activities

**Where to Reach Them:**
- Firm-wide innovation initiatives
- Partner retreats and meetings
- Industry-specific conferences
- Executive peer networks

### 1.2 Secondary Audience

#### Independent Consultants

**Market Size:** 50,000+ independent strategy consultants globally

**Profile:**
- Former MBB or tier-2 consultants who left for independence
- Work on project-based engagements
- No access to firm resources or slide libraries
- Value speed and professional quality

**Pain Points:**
- No access to firm slide templates or libraries
- Doing everything themselves (no analyst support)
- Need to look "big firm" quality to compete
- Limited time for slide polish

**Value Proposition:**
- Access to MBB-quality slides without the firm
- Faster turnaround = more projects possible
- Professional appearance builds credibility

#### In-House Strategy Teams

**Market Size:** 10,000+ corporate strategy teams globally

**Profile:**
- Ex-consultants who moved in-house
- Small teams supporting C-suite
- High expectations but limited resources
- Often disconnected from latest consulting best practices

**Pain Points:**
- Limited slide design resources
- Pressure to deliver consulting-quality output
- Small team managing multiple priorities
- No access to consulting firm IP/methodologies

**Value Proposition:**
- Consulting-quality output without consulting fees
- Standardized, professional presentations
- Access to frameworks and structures

### 2.4 Brand Voice Guidelines

**Tone Attributes:**
- **Professional but approachable:** Like a senior consultant mentoring a junior
- **Confident but not arrogant:** We know consulting; we don't need to prove it
- **Efficient:** Respect the reader's time (short sentences, clear structure)
- **Empathetic:** We understand the late nights and pressure

**Language Dos:**
- Use consulting terminology ("deck," "so what," "MECE")
- Reference consulting culture ("partner review," "steercom")
- Write in active voice
- Use specific numbers ("30 seconds," "5 slides/day")

**Language Don'ts:**
- Avoid buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "disruptive")
- Don't oversell or make unrealistic claims
- Don't sound like a generic SaaS tool
- Avoid overly casual language ("hey," "cool stuff")

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### 3.1 Pricing Tiers

#### Free Tier
**Price:** $0/month  
**Target:** Individual users, discovery/activation

**Features:**
- 5 slides per day
- All slide templates
- Copy to clipboard
- PPTX export
- Basic audience selection
- Email support (community)

**Limitations:**
- Daily usage cap
- No priority generation (standard queue)
- No custom branding
- Limited slide history (7 days)

**Conversion Strategy:**
- Users who hit the 5-slide limit see upgrade prompt
- Show "X slides remaining today" to create scarcity
- Email when approaching limit with upgrade offer

#### Pro Tier
**Price:** $11.99/month (or $99/year - 31% savings)  
**Target:** Power users, independent consultants

**Features:**
- Unlimited slides
- Priority generation (faster processing)
- Unlimited slide history
- Advanced audience options
- Custom color themes
- Priority email support
- Early access to new templates

**ROI Justification:**
- Average consultant rate: $150-300/hour
- Time saved per slide: ~30 minutes
- Break-even: 1 slide saved per month
- Value delivered: 40+ hours/month of slide work saved

#### Team Tier
**Price:** $49/month per user (minimum 3 users)  
**Target:** Boutique consulting firms, in-house strategy teams

**Features:**
- Everything in Pro
- Shared template library
- Team slide history
- Admin dashboard
- Usage analytics
- Custom brand guidelines
- SAML SSO
- Dedicated onboarding
- Priority support

**ROI Justification:**
- Team of 5 consultants: $245/month
- Time saved: 200+ hours/month
- Effective cost: ~$1.20/hour of work saved

#### Enterprise Tier
**Price:** Custom pricing ($500+/month)  
**Target:** Mid-size consulting firms, large in-house teams

**Features:**
- Everything in Team
- Custom AI training on firm style
- Private cloud deployment option
- Advanced security & compliance (SOC 2)
- Custom integrations
- Dedicated account manager
- SLA guarantees
- Custom contract terms

### 3.2 Pricing Psychology & Strategy

**Anchor Pricing:**
- Position against consulting hourly rates ($150-500/hour)
- Pro tier at $11.99 is positioned as "less than a lunch"
- Annual plan creates commitment and reduces churn

**Freemium Strategy:**
- Free tier is genuinely useful (5 slides/day = 150 slides/month)
- Limits designed to create friction for power users
- Upgrade prompts at usage limits, not before

**Competitive Positioning:**
- 50-70% cheaper than Beautiful.ai ($15-20/month)
- More focused/valuable than Canva Pro ($12.99/month)
- Premium to Gamma (free/Pay-as-you-go) but better quality

### 3.3 Promotional Pricing

**Launch Promotion:**
- First 1,000 users: 50% off Pro for first year (promo code: LAUNCH50)
- Creates urgency and early adopter community

**Referral Program:**
- Referrer: 1 month free Pro per successful referral
- Referee: 25% off first month
- Cap at 6 months free (prevents abuse)

**Student/Alumni Pricing:**
- Business school students: Free Pro while enrolled (verified via .edu)
- Recent grads (0-2 years): 50% off Pro
- Builds pipeline from MBA programs
